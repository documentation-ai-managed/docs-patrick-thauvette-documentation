## Overview

Les Armateurs du Saint-Laurent (ASL) unites 14 domestic shipowners who transport goods and passengers across key Canadian waterways. Since 1936, you benefit from ASL's advocacy with governments, officials, and stakeholders. The association drives maritime growth through information sharing, cooperation, promotion, and strong representation.

<Callout kind="info">
ASL pools expertise to monitor regulations, take positions, and forge strategic partnerships that support your operations and the St. Lawrence River's maritime economy.
</Callout>

## Mission and History

ASL's mission centers on promoting sustainable maritime transportation. You rely on ASL to represent shipowners' interests in policy discussions and industry development.

Follow these key milestones in ASL's history:

  <Step title="1936: Founding" icon="anchor">

  </Step>
  <Step title="Post-WWII Expansion" icon="trending-up">

  </Step>
</Steps>
